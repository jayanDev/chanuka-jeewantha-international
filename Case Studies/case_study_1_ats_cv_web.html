The Story
"I had all the experience. I had the skills. But no one was even reading my CV. It felt like shouting into a void."

Amal had spent 9 years building sophisticated backend systems for fintech companies across South Asia. His portfolio was impressive — microservices architecture, cloud migrations, team leadership. By any measure, he was the candidate companies were looking for.

But after being made redundant during a corporate restructure, Amal entered the job market and hit an invisible wall. He was applying to 3–4 senior roles every week. Six months passed. Then seven. The silence was deafening — just two recruiter calls, both from companies far below his level.

A friend referred him to us. He was skeptical. "What can a CV writer do that I haven't already tried?" The answer, it turned out, was everything the ATS was silently rejecting.

What was really happening
🤖
ATS was filtering him out instantly
His CV used job titles and phrasing that didn't match how companies wrote their job descriptions — so the algorithm scored him low before any human saw it.
📄
Achievements buried in duties
Every bullet read like a job description. "Responsible for…" instead of "Reduced API latency by 60% serving 2M+ daily users."
🎯
No targeting — one CV for all roles
A single generic CV was being sent everywhere — no tailoring to role level, company size, or tech stack requirements.
📊
Zero quantification of impact
Nine years of real achievement had no numbers attached — no scale, no cost savings, no performance gains visible on paper.
